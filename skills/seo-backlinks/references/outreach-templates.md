# Outreach Email Templates

Templates pair with the workflow in `link-building-campaign.md`. Personalize every send -- generic outreach is the #1 reason for low response rates.

## Template 1: Guest Post Pitch

**Subject lines (A/B test):**
- "Guest post idea for [Blog Name]"
- "3 content ideas for [Blog Name] readers"
- "Quick question about guest posting"
- "[Mutual Connection] suggested I reach out"

```
Hi [Name],

I've been following [Blog Name] for a while, particularly enjoyed your recent post on [specific article]. The insights on [specific detail] really resonated.

I write about [niche/topic] and thought your audience might benefit from content on [topic area]. A few ideas:

1. [Specific headline idea with hook]
2. [Specific headline idea with hook]
3. [Specific headline idea with hook]

I've written for [Publication 1], [Publication 2], and [Publication 3]. You can see my work here: [portfolio link]

Would any of these topics interest your readers? Happy to adjust to fit your editorial calendar.

Best,
[Your Name]
[Title]
[Website]
```

**Checklist:** recipient's actual name; reference a specific article; mention a specific detail from that article; tailor topic ideas to their audience; include relevant credentials; keep under 150 words.

## Template 2: Resource Page Suggestion

**Subject lines:**
- "Resource suggestion for [Page Title]"
- "Addition to your [Topic] resources?"
- "Found helpful resource for [Page Name]"

```
Hi [Name],

I came across your [Topic] resources page while researching [related topic]:
[URL of their resource page]

I recently created [describe your resource] that covers [specific value]:
[Your resource URL]

It includes [unique feature 1], [unique feature 2], and [unique feature 3] that aren't covered in your current list.

Would this be worth adding to your resources? Happy to provide more details if helpful.

Thanks for maintaining such a useful resource list!

[Your Name]
```

**Best practices:** keep under 100 words; link directly to their resource page; highlight unique value; no hard sell; one focused resource only.

## Template 3: Broken Link Replacement

**Subject lines:**
- "Broken link on [Page Title]"
- "Quick heads up about [Site Name]"
- "Found 404 error on your site"

```
Hi [Name],

I was reading your article "[Article Title]" and noticed a broken link in the [section] section:

Broken URL: [dead link]
Error: 404 / Domain expired / etc.

I actually have a similar resource that covers [same topic]:
[Your URL]

It includes [relevant detail that matches broken content].

Thought you might want to update the link or at least know about the 404.

Either way, great article!

[Your Name]
```

**Follow-up (5 days later):**
```
Hi [Name],

Just following up on the broken link I mentioned. Were you able to update it?

If the replacement I suggested doesn't fit, I'm happy to help find alternatives.

Thanks!
[Your Name]
```

**Tips:** lead with helpfulness, not your link; clearly identify broken link location; explain why your content is a relevant replacement; offer to help regardless of outcome.

## Template 4: Testimonial / Case Study Request

**Subject lines:**
- "Case study opportunity with [Your Company]"
- "Would you share your [Product] results?"
- "Quick testimonial request"

```
Hi [Name],

I noticed you're using [Your Product/Service] for [specific use case].

We're putting together case studies showing how [industry/role] professionals achieve [specific outcome].

Would you be interested in being featured? Benefits include:

- Backlink to your site
- Social media promotion to [X] followers
- Position as industry thought leader
- Free [incentive if applicable]

The process is simple:
1. 15-min interview or written questions
2. We write the case study
3. You review and approve
4. We publish and promote

Interested? I can send questions this week.

Thanks,
[Your Name]
```

**Alternative -- simple testimonial:**
```
Hi [Name],

Thanks for being a [Product] customer! We noticed you've been using [specific feature] for [time period].

Would you mind sharing a quick testimonial about your experience? Just 2-3 sentences on:

- What problem you were solving
- How [Product] helped
- Results you've seen

We'll feature it on our site with a link back to [their site/LinkedIn].

Interested? I can send a simple form or we can do a quick 5-min call.

[Your Name]
```

## Template 5: Digital PR / Data Outreach

**Subject lines:**
- "New data on [trending topic]"
- "[Stat] might interest [Publication] readers"
- "Survey results: [surprising finding]"

```
Hi [Name],

I saw your recent coverage of [related topic] and thought you might be interested in new data we just published.

Key findings from our [survey/study] of [X respondents/data points]:

- [Surprising stat 1]
- [Surprising stat 2]
- [Surprising stat 3]

Full report: [URL]

We have additional data cuts and expert quotes available if you'd like to cover this for [Publication].

Let me know if you need anything!

[Your Name]
[Title]
[Contact]
```

**Journalist tips:** extremely concise; lead with newsworthy angle; provide ready-to-use stats; offer exclusive data or early access; include high-res images/graphics; no ask for backlink (implied).

## Personalization Strategy

### Research checklist (5 min per prospect)

- [ ] Find recipient's name and title
- [ ] Read their last 2-3 articles
- [ ] Note specific details to reference
- [ ] Check LinkedIn for mutual connections
- [ ] Identify their content gaps
- [ ] Confirm email address accuracy

### Mail merge fields

```
{{first_name}} {{site_name}} {{recent_article_url}}
{{recent_article_title}} {{niche}} {{custom_note}}
```

## Subject Line Best Practices

**High-performing patterns:**
- Question format: "Quick question about [topic]?"
- Specificity: "Article idea: [specific headline]"
- Helpfulness: "Found issue on [page name]"
- Name drop: "[Person] suggested I reach out"
- Curiosity: "Noticed you're covering [topic]"

**Avoid:**
- All caps ("GUEST POST OPPORTUNITY")
- Spam triggers ("Free," "Guarantee," "Make money")
- Generic ("Collaboration opportunity")
- Lengthy (>60 characters)
- Clickbait ("You won't believe this...")

**A/B test pattern:** test 2 variants on first 20% of list (Direct/specific vs Curious/question), send winner to remaining 80%.

## Response Handling Templates

**Positive response:**
```
Hi [Name],
Great to hear you're interested!
[Specific next step based on campaign type]
I'll [action item] by [date] and send over [deliverable].
Looking forward to working together!
[Your Name]
```

**Clarification request:**
```
Hi [Name],
Thanks for getting back to me!
To clarify: [answer their question clearly]
[Restate value proposition briefly]
Does that help? Happy to provide more details.
[Your Name]
```

**Delayed response after follow-up:**
```
Hi [Name],
No worries at all! I know you're busy.
[Brief restatement of offer]
Still interested? If timing isn't right, totally understand.
[Your Name]
```

## Tracking Metrics

**Per-template performance:** open rate ≥40%, response rate ≥25%, conversion rate ≥10%, average time to response <48h.

**Optimization signals:**
- Low open rate → test subject lines
- High open, low response → revise body copy
- High response, low conversion → adjust offer/CTA
- Pattern by day/time → adjust send schedule
